## What Sunhub Is

**Sunhub** is America's #1 solar equipment marketplace — a two-sided B2B platform that connects buyers and sellers of new, refurbished, and defective solar equipment across the United States.

Think of it as the eBay/Craigslist for the US solar industry, but built specifically for professionals: EPCs, installers, manufacturers, distributors, and inventory liquidators.

## What They Do

### Core Marketplace
Sunhub runs a searchable marketplace where solar professionals can:
- **Buy** panels, inverters, batteries, EV chargers, DIY kits, solar lights — new, refurbished, or defective
- **Sell** excess inventory, aging stock, or surplus project equipment
- **Source** via RFQ (Request for Quote) — post a buying need, get supplier responses
- **List** via the Sales Portal with offer/quote management tools

### Value to Buyers
- Access thousands of listings from verified sellers across the US
- Find inventory fast — especially large volumes under project deadlines
- Competitive prices vs. traditional distributors
- Free shipping on almost everything
- Brands they know, at lower prices

### Value to Sellers
- Reach qualified buyers at scale without cold outreach
- Move aging inventory before it loses more value
- Capital recovery for manufacturers clearing warehouse overstock
- Sales portal tools to manage offers and quotes

### Industry Intelligence
Sunhub produces content about solar market trends, supply chain insights, and emerging technology (perovskite cells, floating PV, EV batteries). They position themselves as an industry authority, not just a marketplace.

## Target Audience (The 4 Buyer/Seller Personas)

These are the people Sunhub ads must speak to. Every ad should be designed for ONE of these personas — not a generic "solar person."

### 1. EPC Contractors
- **Who:** Engineering, Procurement & Construction firms that build utility-scale or commercial solar installations
- **Age:** 32–55 — project managers, procurement leads, operations directors
- **Pain:** Sourcing large volumes of equipment fast under tight project deadlines. One delayed panel shipment can hold up a multi-million dollar installation. They need reliable bulk supply NOW.
- **What they want from Sunhub:** Speed, volume, reliable sourcing at scale, and RFQ tools that get them competitive quotes without wasting time
- **Ad tone:** Confident, fast, professional. "You need it. We have it. Done."

### 2. Independent Solar Installers
- **Who:** Owner-operators running small-to-mid residential/commercial solar installation businesses
- **Age:** 28–50 — usually the owner wearing every hat
- **Pain:** Can't compete with distributor pricing. Struggle to source locally on short notice. Margin is everything when you're small.
- **What they want from Sunhub:** Better prices than their distributor, fast availability, and the ability to punch above their weight
- **Ad tone:** Empowering, direct, peer-to-peer feel. "Finally, pricing that works for installers like you."

### 3. Solar Manufacturers & Brands
- **Who:** Solar panel and component manufacturers, often with US warehouses or channel sales operations
- **Age:** 35–55 — sales managers, channel managers, regional directors
- **Pain:** Reaching qualified buyers at scale is expensive and slow. Excess inventory sitting in a warehouse is dead capital.
- **What they want from Sunhub:** A ready-made audience of serious buyers, sales tooling to manage quotes and offers, and a way to move product fast
- **Ad tone:** Professional, results-oriented. "Your next customer is already searching here."

### 4. Distributors & Inventory Liquidators
- **Who:** Traditional solar distributors and specialty liquidation companies managing large equipment inventories
- **Age:** 35–60 — operations leads, sales directors, procurement/logistics managers
- **Pain:** Capital tied up in aging stock, price erosion as inventory ages, pressure to move equipment before it's obsolete or loses another 10% of value
- **What they want from Sunhub:** Fast liquidity, buyers who understand equipment condition, and the ability to move volume
- **Ad tone:** Urgent but professional. "Turn aging inventory into cash. Today."

## Brand Identity

### Positioning
- "America's No. 1 Solar Marketplace"
- Professional, energetic, trustworthy
- The platform that makes solar procurement fast and efficient — for both sides of the transaction

### Voice & Tone
- **Direct** — solar professionals don't have time for fluff
- **Confident** — Sunhub is the market leader, not a startup asking for trust
- **Energetic** — the brand color is Blaze Orange for a reason — urgency, momentum, action
- **Knowledgeable** — they publish market intelligence, they know the industry

### Brand Colors
| Color | Hex | Role |
|-------|-----|------|
| Blaze Orange | `#FF6B00` | Primary CTA, hero backgrounds, energy |
| Web Orange / Gold | `#FFC600` | Secondary highlights, warmth, badges |
| Big Stone (Dark Navy) | `#1A2B38` | Headers, trust, corporate gravitas |
| Black | `#000000` | Primary text |
| Heather (Light Blue-Gray) | `#DAE1E5` | Subtle backgrounds, borders |
| Azure (Medium Blue) | `#3F7AB5` | Informational, secondary actions |
| White | `#FFFFFF` | Backgrounds, reversed text |

**Important:** Orange is the brand's dominant energy color but should NOT appear in every ad mechanically. Some ads call for the dark navy gravitas, some for pure white professional cleanness. Use color intentionally per concept.

### Typography
- **Primary:** Poppins — geometric sans-serif, clean, modern, professional
- **Fallback:** Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif

### Visual Style
- Clean, bold, high-contrast — not cluttered
- Strong hero imagery over dark overlays with white or orange text
- Photography > illustration — real solar installations, real professionals, real equipment
- **Never**: cheesy stock solar clipart, generic "sun" graphics, split-screen composites
- **Solar panels in photography**: Matte black / dark gray only. Never blue. Blue solar panels are old technology and misrepresent the product.
- Hyperrealistic photography style — real textures, real light, real environments
- Professional settings: construction sites, warehouse floors, rooftops, commercial buildings, open fields with ground-mount arrays

## Industry Context the Agent Should Know

- The US solar market has experienced significant supply chain volatility — prices swung sharply, tariffs changed, and excess inventory built up in distributor warehouses
- Many EPCs and installers pivoted to sourcing refurbished or distressed inventory to stay cost-competitive
- Manufacturers face pressure from Chinese competition and often need to move US-warehoused stock fast
- Solar panels degrade in perceived value even if physically fine — "aging stock" stigma is real
- The shift from blue polycrystalline to black monocrystalline panels is complete — the industry runs on black panels now
- EV chargers and batteries are adjacent growth categories where the same buyer relationships apply
- Most solar B2B purchasing is still done over phone/email — Sunhub's digital marketplace is a genuine modernization play

## What NOT to Design

- Generic "solar future" aspirational lifestyle ads with no specific audience
- Blue solar panels (ever)
- Split-screen before/after compositions
- Cartoons, icons, or flat illustrations as the primary visual
- Ads that don't have a clear persona target — every concept must be for a specific one of the 4 audiences
- Over-branded orange on every single ad — use the full palette intentionally
